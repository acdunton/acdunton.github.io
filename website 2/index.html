Academic Profile

My key research interests are modern and contemporary art, with particular attention to valuing works. In January of 2017, I will be undertaking an internship module at the Tate Modern that will focus on the priorities and policies of museums in the future. I have an excellent academic record; graduating from secondary school with the highest IB score in my year group and an undergraduate essay written in French was personally selected by the Director of French Studies to represent the University of Leeds at a national competition.

Interests

The Art Economy Independent research.
I can demonstrate extensive knowledge of the globalised market. I take care to move beyond a eurocentric fixation with established dealers and auction houses, instead focussing on developping markets in Russia and Asia. What fascinates me most is the valuation of contemporary works and corporeate art colllections.

Cultural Capital Dissertation Project.
Bourdieu's theory of Cultural Capital that refers to a collection of symbolic 'cultural' elements that are connected to prestige and social standing. This is an increasingly pertinent concept to today's issues of access and power within the creative industires.

Languages Self-improvement.
Within the context of the globalised creative economy, languages are an essential aspect to the expansion of the art market. I currently speak fluent (C2) French and have a limitied working proficiency in Spanish (B1). I am working to move beyond a latin, european language scope and in the near future I hope to learn Russian and Japanese.
